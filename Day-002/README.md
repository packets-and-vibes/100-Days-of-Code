## What I Learned
- The act of getting the index of "Hello"[1] is called "subscripting." I knew how to do it, but I didn't know the term.
- Humans often put commas in large numbers, example: 1,000,000. In Python, we can't use commas in numbers, but we can use underscores to make the number more reader-friendly to humans, exmple: 1_000_000

## Challenges
This was surprisingly challenging. I miscalculated the tip amount a few times and tried to pack too many operations into a single calculation. Since I'm new and still learning, I decided to break it down into multiple variables so I could use print statements along the way to check my work.
One thing I noticed is that the round() function didn't work exactly as I had expected. I did round(pay_per_friend, 2) and when the total was 33.60, the output was 33.6. I thought that my rounding was incorrect, but it must be a default to not add unnecessary zeros in rounding. I retried with different values and got the two decimal places that I was expecting.
